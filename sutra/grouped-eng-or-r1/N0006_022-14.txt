4. The Burning Embers Chapter  

136. First: Burning Embers  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, form is burning embers, feeling is burning embers, perception is burning embers, formations are burning embers, consciousness is burning embers.  

4. Bhikkhus, when a noble disciple who has heard this sees thus, they become disenchanted with form, disenchanted with feeling... perception... formations... consciousness.  

5. Being disenchanted, they become dispassionate... [and] understanding... they no longer return to renewed existence."  

137. Second: Impermanent (1)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, desire should be abandoned for what is impermanent. What is impermanent?  

4-6. Form is impermanent; desire for this should be abandoned. Feeling... perception... formations...  

7. Consciousness is impermanent; desire for this should be abandoned.  

8. Bhikkhus, desire should be abandoned for what is impermanent."  

138. Third: Impermanent (2)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, lust should be abandoned for what is impermanent. What is impermanent?  

4-7. Form is impermanent; lust for this should be abandoned. Feeling... perception... formations... Consciousness is impermanent; lust for this should be abandoned.  

8. Bhikkhus, lust should be abandoned for what is impermanent."  

139. Fourth: Impermanent (3)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, craving should be abandoned for what is impermanent. What is impermanent?  

4-7. Form is impermanent; craving for this should be abandoned. Feeling... perception... formations... Consciousness is impermanent; craving for this should be abandoned.  

8. Bhikkhus, craving should be abandoned for what is impermanent."  

140-142. Sixth-Seventh: Suffering (1-3)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3-8. "Bhikkhus, desire... lust... craving... should be abandoned for what is suffering... and so on..."  

143-145. Eighth-Tenth: Not-Self (1-3)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, desire... lust... craving... should be abandoned for what is not-self. What is not-self?  

4-6. Form is not-self; desire... lust... craving... for this should be abandoned. Feeling... perception... formations...  

7. Consciousness is not-self; desire... lust... craving... for this should be abandoned.  

8. Bhikkhus, desire... lust... craving... should be abandoned for what is not-self."  

146. Eleventh: The Noble Son's Suffering (1)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, a noble son who has gone forth out of faith considers this fitting: dwelling much disenchanted with form, with feeling... perception... formations... consciousness.  

4. Dwelling thus disenchanted, he fully understands form... feeling... perception... formations... consciousness.  

5. Having fully understood them, he is liberated from form, feeling, perception, formations, consciousness; liberated from birth, aging, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and despair; liberated from suffering."  

147. Twelfth: The Noble Son's Suffering (2)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, a noble son who has gone forth out of faith considers this fitting: dwelling contemplating impermanence in form... feeling... perception... formations... consciousness... and so on...  

4-5. ...is liberated from suffering."  

148. Thirteenth: The Noble Son's Suffering (3)  

1-2. [Thus have I heard. At one time] the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, a noble son who has gone forth out of faith considers this fitting: dwelling contemplating not-self in form... feeling... perception... formations... consciousness.  

4. Dwelling thus, he fully understands form... feeling... perception... formations... consciousness.  

5. Having fully understood them, he is liberated from form, feeling, perception, formations, consciousness; liberated from birth, aging, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, distress, and despair; liberated from suffering."  

End of the Burning Embers Chapter.  

Its summary verses:  
Burning Embers, three on the impermanent,  
Three on suffering, three on not-self,  
And these threefold teachings  
On the noble son's suffering thrice.

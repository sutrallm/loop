Chapter 2  
[12] 1. The Unconditioned  
1. Cessation  
1. "Bhikkhus, I will teach you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned. Listen attentively.  
2. Bhikkhus, what is the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, the destruction of greed, the destruction of hatred, the destruction of delusion. Bhikkhus, this is called the unconditioned.  
3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, cessation—this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.  
4. Bhikkhus, thus I have taught you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned.  
5. Bhikkhus, whatever a teacher who has compassion and desires the welfare of his disciples should do out of compassion, all that I have done for you.  
6. Bhikkhus, contemplate these roots of trees, these empty dwellings. Do not be negligent; do not later face regret. This is my instruction to you."  

2. Observation  
3. "Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, observation—this is called the path leading to the unconditioned."  

3. Six Kinds of Samadhi  
※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, samadhi with initial and sustained thought—this is called the path leading to the unconditioned. ...(1)  
4. ※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Samadhi without initial but with sustained thought. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned. ...(2)  
5. ※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Samadhi without initial or sustained thought. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned. ...(3)  
6. ※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Empty samadhi. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned. ...(4)  
7. ※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Signless samadhi. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned. ...(5)  
8. ※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Wishless samadhi. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned. ...(6)  

9–12. Four Foundations of Mindfulness  
※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, here a bhikkhu dwells contemplating the body in the body (1)... dwells contemplating feelings in feelings (2)... dwells contemplating mind in mind (3)... dwells contemplating phenomena in phenomena (4)—ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and aversion toward the world. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.  

13–16. Four Right Efforts  
※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, here a bhikkhu generates desire, strives, activates energy, applies mind, and exerts to prevent unarisen evil unwholesome states from arising (1)... to abandon arisen evil unwholesome states (2)... to arouse unarisen wholesome states (3)... to maintain, prevent decline, increase, expand, and fulfill arisen wholesome states (4). Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.  

17–20. Four Bases of Spiritual Power  
※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, here a bhikkhu develops the basis of spiritual power endowed with concentration through desire and exertion (1)... through energy and exertion (2)... through mind and exertion (3)... through investigation and exertion (4). Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.  

21–25. Five Faculties  
※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, here a bhikkhu develops the faculty of faith (1)... energy (2)... mindfulness (3)... concentration (4)... wisdom (5), which leads to renunciation, dispassion, and cessation. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.  

26–30. Five Powers  
※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, here a bhikkhu develops the power of faith (1)... energy (2)... mindfulness (3)... concentration (4)... wisdom (5), which leads to renunciation, dispassion, and cessation. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.  

31–37. Seven Factors of Enlightenment  
※3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, here a bhikkhu develops the enlightenment factor of mindfulness (1)... discrimination of phenomena (2)... energy (3)... joy (4)... tranquility (5)... concentration (6)... equanimity (7), which leads to renunciation, dispassion, and cessation. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned."  

38–45. Noble Eightfold Path  
1. "Bhikkhus, I will teach you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned. Listen attentively.  
2. Bhikkhus, what is the unconditioned? The destruction of greed, hatred, and delusion. Bhikkhus, this is called the unconditioned.  
3. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Bhikkhus, here a bhikkhu develops right view (1)... right intention (2)... right speech (3)... right action (4)... right livelihood (5)... right effort (6)... right mindfulness (7)... right concentration (8), which leads to renunciation, dispassion, and cessation. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the unconditioned.  
4. Bhikkhus, thus I have taught you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned.  
5. Bhikkhus, whatever a teacher who has compassion and desires the welfare of his disciples should do out of compassion, all that I have done for you.  
6. Bhikkhus, contemplate these roots of trees, these empty dwellings. Do not be negligent; do not later face regret. This is my instruction to you."

Summary Verses:  
The unconditioned, the ultimate, the non-outflowing,  
Truth, the far shore, skillfulness, the hard-to-see,  
Non-aging, secure, clarity, the incomparable,  
Non-conceptual, peace, nectar, the sublime,  
Tranquility, safety, end of craving, the marvelous,  
Unprecedented, calamity-free, calamity-free Dharma,  
Nirvana, undiminished, dispassion, purity,  
Liberation, non-abiding, a lamp, shelter, refuge,  
Crossing over—such are the states the Tathagata taught.

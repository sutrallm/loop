10. The Chapter on Heedfulness (Detachment) [89–98] 1–10  

1. O monks! Among beings without feet, two feet, four feet, or many feet—whether with form or formless, with perception or without perception—the Tathagata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, is proclaimed supreme. O monks! Similarly, all wholesome dharma are rooted in heedfulness, converge in heedfulness, and heedfulness is declared the foremost of all dharma. ... and so it is extensively taught ...  

The summary verse states:  
The Tathagata and footprints,  
The roof and the root-core,  
Summer growth, flower, king, moon,  
Sun, robe, and ten such phrases.

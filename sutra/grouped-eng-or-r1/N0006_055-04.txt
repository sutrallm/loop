4. The Chapter on the Moistening of Merit  

31. 1. Moistening (1)  
1. At Sāvatthī.  
2. "Bhikkhus, there are four kinds of moistening of merit, wholesome moistening, sustenance of happiness. What are the four?  
3. Here, bhikkhus, a noble disciple possesses verified faith in the Buddha, thus: 'That Blessed One is an arhat, perfectly enlightened, accomplished in true knowledge and conduct, fortunate, knower of the world, unsurpassed trainer of persons to be tamed, teacher of gods and humans, the Enlightened One, the Blessed One.' This is the first moistening of merit, wholesome moistening, sustenance of happiness.  
4. Again, bhikkhus, a noble disciple possesses verified faith in the Dhamma, thus: 'The Dhamma of the Blessed One is well-expounded, directly visible, immediate, inviting one to come and see, applicable, to be personally realized by the wise.' This is the second moistening of merit...  
5. Again, bhikkhus, a noble disciple possesses verified faith in the Saṅgha, thus: 'The Saṅgha of the Blessed One’s disciples is practicing the good way, practicing the straight way, practicing the true way, practicing the proper way; that is, the four pairs of persons, the eight types of individuals. This Saṅgha is worthy of gifts, hospitality, offerings, and reverential salutations, the unsurpassed field of merit for the world.' This is the third moistening of merit...  
6. Again, bhikkhus, a noble disciple possesses virtues that are unbroken, untorn, unblemished, unmottled, freeing, praised by the wise, ungrasped, leading to samādhi.  
7. This is the fourth moistening of merit..."  

32. 2. Moistening (2)  
2. "Bhikkhus, there are four kinds of moistening of merit...  
3. Here, a noble disciple possesses verified faith in the Buddha...  
4-5. Again, a noble disciple... in the Dhamma... in the Saṅgha...  
6. Again, a noble disciple dwells at home with a heart free from the stain of miserliness, freely generous, open-handed, delighting in relinquishment, devoted to charity, rejoicing in giving.  
7. This is the fourth moistening of merit..."  

33. 3. Moistening (3)  
2. "Bhikkhus, there are four kinds...  
3-5. Here, a noble disciple... verified faith in the Buddha... Dhamma... Saṅgha...  
6. He is wise, possessing noble discernment leading to the complete destruction of suffering, endowed with the knowledge of arising and passing away.  
7. This is the fourth moistening of merit..."  

34. 4. Heavenly Path (1)  
1. At Sāvatthī.  
2. "Bhikkhus, there are four heavenly paths that purify unpurified beings and cleanse unblemished beings. What are the four?  
3. Here, a noble disciple possesses verified faith in the Buddha... This is the first heavenly path...  
4-6. ...in the Dhamma... in the Saṅgha... possesses virtues... leading to samādhi.  
7. These four heavenly paths purify unpurified beings and cleanse unblemished beings."  

35. 5. Heavenly Path (2)  
2. "Bhikkhus, there are four heavenly paths...  
3. Here, a noble disciple... reflects: 'What is the heavenly path? The gods regard non-anger as supreme. I too, whether fearful or secure, will not harbor anger...'  
4-6. ...in the Dhamma... Saṅgha... virtues...  
7. These four heavenly paths purify..."  

36. 6. Companionship  
2. "Bhikkhus, when one possesses four qualities, the gods rejoice and claim them as companions. What four?  
3-6. Verified faith in the Buddha... Dhamma... Saṅgha... virtues...  
7. The gods, having such virtues, rejoice and claim them as companions."  

37. 7. Mahānāma  
1. At Kapilavatthu.  
2. Mahānāma asked: "What is a lay follower?"  
3. "One who has gone for refuge to the Buddha, Dhamma, Saṅgha."  
4. "What is virtue?"  
5. "Abstaining from killing... false speech... alcohol."  
6. "What is faith?"  
7. "Faith in the Buddha’s enlightenment..."  
8. "What is generosity?"  
9. "Giving freely without stinginess..."  
10. "What is wisdom?"  
11. "Wisdom leading to the destruction of suffering..."  

38. 8. Rain  
2. "Bhikkhus, just as rainwater flows downstream, filling rivers until the ocean swells, so does a noble disciple’s faith in the Buddha, Dhamma, Saṅgha, and virtues lead to the destruction of defilements."  

39. 9. Sāḷhā  
1. At Kapilavatthu.  
3. The Buddha taught Sāḷhā the four factors of stream-entry: faith, generosity, virtue, and wisdom.  
5. Sāḷhā affirmed her possession of these qualities.  
6. The Buddha declared her a stream-enterer.  

40. 10. Nandiya  
1. The Buddha explained the difference between heedless and heedful disciples.  
5-6. Heedlessness leads to stagnation; heedfulness leads to progress through joy, ease, concentration, and insight.  

End of the Chapter on the Moistening of Merit  
Verses of Compilation:  
Three Moistening sections, two Heavenly Paths, Companionship, Mahānāma, Rain, Sāḷhā, Nandiya.

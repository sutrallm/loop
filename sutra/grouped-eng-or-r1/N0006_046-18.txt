18. Floods (Subduing Desire)  

[166–174] 1–9  

1. At Savatthi.  

2. Bhikkhus, there are four floods. What four? The flood of sensual desire, the flood of existence, the flood of views, and the flood of ignorance. ... This is spoken in detail.  

10. [175] The Higher Fetters  

2. Bhikkhus, there are five higher fetters. What five? Lust for form, lust for the formless, conceit, restlessness, and ignorance. These are the five higher fetters.  

3. Bhikkhus, to directly know, fully understand, completely destroy, and abandon these five higher fetters, seven factors of enlightenment should be cultivated. What seven?  

4. Here, a bhikkhu develops the enlightenment factor of mindfulness, subduing and exhausting sensual desire, subduing and exhausting ill will, subduing and exhausting delusion ... inclining toward deathlessness, tending toward deathlessness, entering deathlessness ... inclining toward Nibbāna, tending toward Nibbāna, entering Nibbāna. He develops the enlightenment factor of discernment ... the enlightenment factor of energy ... the enlightenment factor of rapture ... the enlightenment factor of tranquility ... the enlightenment factor of concentration ... the enlightenment factor of equanimity.  

5. Bhikkhus, to directly know, fully understand, completely destroy, and abandon these five higher fetters, these seven factors of enlightenment should be cultivated.  

Summary Verses:  
Floods, yokes, grasping,  
Bonds, latent tendencies, sensual pleasures,  
Hindrances, aggregates, lower and higher fetters.  

It should be understood that this chapter is for the complete destruction of desire, complete destruction of ill will, complete destruction of delusion.

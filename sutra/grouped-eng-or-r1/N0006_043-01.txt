**Chapter 1**

(1) First: The Body  

1. "Monks, I will teach you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned. Listen attentively.  
2. Monks, what is the unconditioned? Monks, the destruction of greed, the destruction of hatred, the destruction of delusion. Monks, this is called the unconditioned.  
3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Mindfulness directed toward the body. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  
4. Thus, monks, I have taught you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned.  
5. Monks, whatever a teacher who desires the welfare of his disciples, out of compassion, should do for them, that I have done for you.  
6. Monks, dwell contemplating these trees and empty dwellings. Do not be negligent, and do not incur regret later. This is my instruction to you."  

(2) Second: Calm and Insight  

1. "Monks, I will teach you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned. Listen attentively.  
2. Monks, what is the unconditioned? Monks, the destruction of greed, the destruction of hatred, the destruction of delusion. Monks, this is called the unconditioned.  
3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Calm and insight. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned."  

(3) Third: Contemplation with Thought  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Meditative concentration with thought and examination, meditative concentration without thought but with examination, meditative concentration without thought or examination. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(4) Fourth: Emptiness  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? Meditative concentration on emptiness, meditative concentration on the signless, meditative concentration on the desireless. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(5) Fifth: Foundations of Mindfulness  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? The four foundations of mindfulness. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(6) Sixth: Right Strivings  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? The four right strivings. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(7) Seventh: Bases of Spiritual Power  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? The four bases of spiritual power. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(8) Eighth: Faculties  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? The five faculties. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(9) Ninth: Powers  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? The five powers. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(10) Tenth: Factors of Enlightenment  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? The seven factors of enlightenment. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  

(11) Eleventh: The Path  

3. Monks, what is the path leading to the unconditioned? The Noble Eightfold Path. Monks, this is called the path to the unconditioned.  
4. Thus, monks, I have taught you the unconditioned and the path leading to the unconditioned.  
5. Monks, whatever a teacher who desires the welfare of his disciples, out of compassion, should do for them, that I have done for you.  
6. Monks, dwell contemplating these trees and empty dwellings. Do not be negligent, and do not incur regret later. This is my instruction to you."  

**The First Chapter on Nirvana (End)**  

The summary verses say: Body, meditative attainments with thought, emptiness, foundations of mindfulness, right strivings, bases of spiritual power, faculties, powers, factors of enlightenment, and the path as the eleventh.

8. Dragon Corresponding  

[1] The First: Categories  

1-2  
Thus have I heard. At one time, the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3  
"Monks, there are four kinds of dragons born. What are the four? Egg-born dragons, womb-born dragons, moisture-born dragons, and spontaneously-born dragons. Monks, these are the four kinds of dragons born."  

[2] The Second: Splendid  

1-3  
...At that time, the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi... (An egg-born dragon, a womb-born dragon, a moisture-born dragon,) a spontaneously-born dragon.  

4  
"Monks, among these, womb-born, moisture-born, and spontaneously-born dragons are considered more splendid than egg-born dragons.  

5  
Monks, among these, moisture-born and spontaneously-born dragons are considered more splendid than egg-born and womb-born dragons.  

6  
Monks, among these, spontaneously-born dragons are considered more splendid than egg-born, womb-born, and moisture-born dragons.  

7  
Monks, these are the four kinds of dragons born."  

[3] The Third: Uposatha (1)  

1  
At that time, the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

2  
Then a monk approached the Blessed One’s dwelling. Having approached, he paid homage to the Blessed One and sat down to one side.  

3  
Seated to one side, that monk said to the Blessed One:  

"Venerable Sir, what is the cause, what is the condition, why here a certain egg-born dragon observes Uposatha and relinquishes its own body?"  

4  
"Monk, here a certain egg-born dragon thinks: ‘Previously, we performed both wholesome and unwholesome deeds with body, speech, and mind. Having done so, after death, we were reborn among egg-born dragons.  

5  
Now, if we practice wholesome deeds with body, speech, and mind, then after death, we will be reborn in a good destination, the heavenly realm.  

6  
Let us practice wholesome deeds with body, speech, and mind.’  

7  
Monk, this is the cause, this is the condition, why here a certain egg-born dragon observes Uposatha and relinquishes its own body."

[7] The Seventh: Hearing (1)  

1-2  
...At that time, the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

3  
Seated to one side, that monk said to the Blessed One:  

"Venerable Sir, what is the cause, what is the condition, why here a certain being, after death, is reborn among egg-born dragons?"  

4  
"Monk, here a certain being performs both wholesome and unwholesome deeds with body, speech, and mind. They hear that egg-born dragons are long-lived, beautiful, and experience much pleasure.  

5  
They think: ‘After death, may I be reborn among egg-born dragons!’  

6  
After death, they are reborn among egg-born dragons.  

7  
Monk, this is the cause, this is the condition, why here a certain being, after death, is reborn among egg-born dragons."

[11-20] The Eleventh-Twentieth: The Benefit of Giving (1-4)  

1-3  
...At that time, the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi...  

Seated to one side, that monk said to the Blessed One:  

"Venerable Sir, what is the cause, what is the condition, why here a certain being, after death, is reborn among egg-born dragons... womb-born dragons... moisture-born dragons... spontaneously-born dragons?"  

4  
"Monk, here a certain being performs both wholesome and unwholesome deeds with body, speech, and mind. They hear that (egg-born/womb-born/moisture-born/spontaneously-born) dragons are long-lived, beautiful, and experience much pleasure.  

5  
They think: ‘After death, may I be reborn among (egg-born/womb-born/moisture-born/spontaneously-born) dragons!’  

6  
They give food, drink, clothing, vehicles, garlands, perfumes, unguents, bedding, dwellings, and lamps. After death, they are reborn among (egg-born/womb-born/moisture-born/spontaneously-born) dragons.  

7  
Monk, this is the cause, this is the condition, why here a certain being, after death, is reborn among (egg-born/womb-born/moisture-born/spontaneously-born) dragons."  

(Thus, four categories are explained in detail through forty discourses, with ten sections and fifty total discourses.)  

The Dragon Corresponding (End)  

Summary Verses:  
Categories and Splendid,  
Four Uposathas, Four Hearings,  
Four Benefits of Giving—  
Thus is the noble teaching on dragons.

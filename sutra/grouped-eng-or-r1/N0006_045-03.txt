3. Evil Nature Chapter  
(21) 1. Evil Nature  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī.  
3.  
"Monks, I will teach you about wrong nature and right nature. Listen carefully.  
4.  
Monks, what is wrong nature? It is wrong view, [wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness,] wrong concentration. Monks, this is called wrong nature.  
5.  
Monks, what is right nature? It is right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. Monks, this is called right nature."  

(22) 2. Unwholesome Qualities  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī.  
...  
3.  
"Monks, I will teach you about unwholesome qualities and wholesome qualities. Listen carefully.  
4.  
Monks, what are unwholesome qualities? They are wrong view, [wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness,] wrong concentration. Monks, these are called unwholesome qualities.  
5.  
[Monks,] what are wholesome qualities? [They are] right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. [Monks,] these [are called] wholesome qualities."  

(23) 3. Path (1)  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
"Monks, I will teach you about the wrong path and the right path. Listen carefully.  
4.  
Monks, what is the wrong path? It is wrong view, [wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness,] wrong concentration. Monks, this is called the wrong path.  
5.  
Monks, what is the right path? It is right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. Monks, this is called the right path."  

(24) 4. Path (2)  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
"Monks, I do not praise wrong paths, whether for laypeople or monastics.  
4.  
Monks, laypeople or monastics who follow wrong paths, due to their wrong conduct, do not incline toward wholesome qualities in accordance with truth. Monks, what is the wrong path? It is wrong view, [wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness,] wrong concentration. Monks, this is called the wrong path. Monks, I do not praise wrong paths for laypeople or monastics.  
5.  
Monks, laypeople or monastics who follow wrong paths, due to their wrong conduct, do not incline toward wholesome qualities in accordance with truth.  
6.  
Monks, I praise right paths for laypeople and monastics.  
7.  
Monks, laypeople or monastics who follow right paths, due to their right conduct, incline toward wholesome qualities in accordance with truth. Monks, what is the right path? It is right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. Monks, this is called the right path. Monks, I praise right paths for laypeople and monastics.  
8.  
Monks, laypeople or monastics who follow right paths, due to their right conduct, incline toward wholesome qualities in accordance with truth."  

(25) 5. Unwholesome Person (1)  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
"Monks, I will teach you about unwholesome and wholesome persons. Listen carefully.  
4.  
Monks, what is an unwholesome person? Here, someone possesses wrong view, [wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness,] wrong concentration. Monks, this is called an unwholesome person.  
5.  
Monks, what is a wholesome person? Here, someone possesses right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. Monks, this is called a wholesome person."  

(26) 6. Unwholesome Person (2)  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
"Monks, I will teach you about someone worse than an unwholesome person and someone superior to a wholesome person. Listen carefully.  
4.  
Monks, what is an unwholesome person? Here, someone possesses wrong view, [wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness,] wrong concentration. Monks, this is called an unwholesome person.  
5.  
Monks, what is someone worse than an unwholesome person? Here, someone possesses wrong view, [wrong thought, wrong speech, wrong action, wrong livelihood, wrong effort, wrong mindfulness,] wrong concentration, wrong knowledge, and wrong liberation. Monks, this is called someone worse than an unwholesome person.  
6.  
Monks, what is a wholesome person? Here, someone possesses right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. Monks, this is called a wholesome person.  
7.  
Monks, what is someone superior to a wholesome person? Here, someone possesses right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration, right knowledge, and right liberation. Monks, this is called someone superior to a wholesome person."  

(27) 7. The Pot  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
"Monks, just as a pot without a stand is easily toppled, but one with a stand is hard to topple, so too, the mind without a support is easily shaken, but with a support, it is hard to shake.  
4.  
Monks, what is the mind’s support? It is the Noble Eightfold Path: right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. This is the mind’s support.  
5.  
Monks, just as a pot without a stand is easily toppled, but one with a stand is hard to topple, so too, the mind without a support is easily shaken, but with a support, it is hard to shake."  

(28) 8. Concentration  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
"Monks, I will teach you about noble right concentration with its supports and prerequisites. Listen carefully.  
4.  
Monks, what is noble right concentration with its supports and prerequisites? It is right view, right thought, [right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness.]  
5.  
Monks, the unification of mind equipped with these seven factors is the prerequisite. Monks, this is called noble right concentration with its supports and prerequisites."  

(29) 9. Feelings  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
"Monks, there are three kinds of feelings. What are the three? Pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neutral feeling. Monks, these are the three feelings.  
4.  
Monks, to fully understand these three feelings, cultivate the Noble Eightfold Path. What is the Noble Eightfold Path? It is right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. Monks, to fully understand these feelings, cultivate this Noble Eightfold Path."  

(30) 10. Udāyī  
1-2.  
At Sāvatthī...  
3.  
Then Venerable Udāyī approached the Blessed One’s dwelling. [Having approached, he paid homage and sat down to one side.]  
4.  
Sitting to one side, Venerable Udāyī said to the Blessed One:  
"Venerable Sir, while in seclusion, this thought arose: ‘May the Blessed One explain the five cords of sensual pleasure.’ What, Venerable Sir, are the five cords of sensual pleasure?"  
5.  
"Good, good, Udāyī! I describe these five cords of sensual pleasure: forms cognized by the eye—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, enticing, and lustful; sounds cognized by the ear [—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, enticing, and lustful]; smells cognized by the nose [—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, enticing, and lustful]; tastes cognized by the tongue [—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, enticing, and lustful]; tactile sensations cognized by the body—desirable, lovely, agreeable, pleasing, enticing, and lustful. Udāyī, these are the five cords of sensual pleasure.  
6.  
Udāyī, to abandon these five cords of sensual pleasure, cultivate the Noble Eightfold Path. What is the Noble Eightfold Path? It is right view, [right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness,] right concentration. Udāyī, to abandon these five cords of sensual pleasure, cultivate this Noble Eightfold Path."  

End of the Evil Nature Chapter.  

The summary verses of this chapter:  
Evil nature and unwholesome,  
Two paths, one wholesome person,  
The pot’s support and concentration,  
Feelings, and Udāyī.

13. The Floods (Detachment)  
[111–119] 1st–9th  

1. Savatthi...  

2. Bhikkhus, there are four floods. What are these four? The flood of sensual desire, the flood of existence, the flood of views, and the flood of ignorance... as is fully taught...  

[120] 10. Higher Fetters  

2. Bhikkhus, there are five higher fetters. What are these five? Craving for form, craving for the formless, conceit, restlessness, and ignorance. These, bhikkhus, are the five higher fetters.  

3. Bhikkhus, to fully understand... thoroughly comprehend... completely destroy... and abandon these five higher fetters, seven factors of enlightenment should be cultivated. What are these seven factors of enlightenment?  

4. Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu cultivates the mindfulness enlightenment factor through detachment, dispassion, cessation, and relinquishment... [the investigation of states enlightenment factor... the effort enlightenment factor... the joy enlightenment factor... the tranquility enlightenment factor... the concentration enlightenment factor...] through detachment, dispassion, cessation, and relinquishment, he cultivates the equanimity enlightenment factor.  

5. Bhikkhus, to fully understand... thoroughly comprehend... completely destroy... and abandon these five higher fetters, these seven factors of enlightenment should be cultivated.  

Summary verse states:  
Floods, yokes, grasping,  
Bonds, latent tendencies, sensual pleasures,  
Hindrances, aggregates, lower and higher fetters.

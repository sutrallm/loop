10. The Floods Chapter * [94–103] 1–9. 10. Higher Fetters. *2. "Bhikkhus, there are five higher fetters. What are the five? Lust for form, lust for the formless, conceit, restlessness, and ignorance. These are the five higher fetters. 3. Bhikkhus, to directly know... fully understand... completely eradicate... abandon these five higher fetters, one should develop the four establishments of mindfulness. What are the four? 4. Here, a bhikkhu dwells contemplating the body in the body, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure toward the world; contemplating feelings... mind... phenomena in phenomena, ardent, clearly comprehending, mindful, having removed covetousness and displeasure toward the world. 5. Bhikkhus, to directly know... fully understand... completely eradicate... abandon these five higher fetters, one should develop these four establishments of mindfulness." The summary verses: Floods, yokes, attachments, bonds, latent tendencies, sensual pleasures, hindrances, aggregates, lower and higher fetters. The Floods Chapter (End). This chapter should be expounded in detail by the establishments of mindfulness, as thoroughly taught in the Way and the Mindfulness Corresponding. Third Mindfulness Corresponding (End).

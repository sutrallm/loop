4. The Unheard Chapter  

31. First: Unheard  

1-2. At Savatthi.  

3. "Bhikkhus, when I considered: 'Contemplating the body in the body,' there arose in me qualities unseen before: vision arose, knowledge arose, understanding arose, light arose. When I considered: 'This establishment of mindfulness regarding the body should be cultivated... it has been cultivated,' there arose in me qualities unseen before: vision arose, knowledge arose, understanding arose, light arose.  

4. 'Contemplating feelings in feelings...'  

5. 'Contemplating mind in mind...'  

6. 'Contemplating phenomena in phenomena... This establishment of mindfulness regarding phenomena should be cultivated... it has been cultivated,' there arose in me qualities unseen before: vision arose, knowledge arose, understanding arose, light arose."  

32. Second: Dispassion  

1-2. At Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, the cultivation and frequent practice of the four establishments of mindfulness leads to complete disenchantment, dispassion, cessation, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, and Nibbāna. What are the four?  

4. Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu contemplates the body in the body, ardent, fully aware, and mindful, having subdued worldly covetousness and discontent; he contemplates feelings in feelings... mind in mind... phenomena in phenomena, ardent, fully aware, and mindful, having subdued worldly covetousness and discontent.  

5. Bhikkhus, the cultivation and frequent practice of these four establishments of mindfulness leads to complete disenchantment, dispassion, cessation, peace, direct knowledge, enlightenment, and Nibbāna."  

33. Third: Loss  

3. "Bhikkhus, one who neglects the four establishments of mindfulness neglects the noble path leading to the complete destruction of suffering.  

4. Bhikkhus, one who undertakes the four establishments of mindfulness undertakes the noble path leading to the complete destruction of suffering. What are the four?  

5. Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu contemplates the body in the body [ardent, fully aware, and mindful...]; feelings... mind... phenomena in phenomena, ardent, fully aware, and mindful, having subdued worldly covetousness and discontent.  

6. Bhikkhus, one who neglects these four establishments of mindfulness neglects the noble path leading to the complete destruction of suffering. One who undertakes them undertakes the noble path."  

34. Fourth: Cultivation  

1-2. At Savatthi...  

3. Bhikkhus, the cultivation and frequent practice of the four establishments of mindfulness leads from this shore to the far shore. What are the four?  

4. Here, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu contemplates the body in the body [ardent...]... phenomena in phenomena, ardent...  

5. Bhikkhus, the cultivation and frequent practice of these four establishments of mindfulness leads from this shore to the far shore."  

35. Fifth: Mindfulness  

1-2. At Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, dwell with clear comprehension and mindfulness—this is my instruction to you.  

4. How is a bhikkhu mindful? Here, a bhikkhu contemplates the body in the body, ardent... He contemplates feelings... mind... phenomena... Thus, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is mindful.  

5. How is a bhikkhu clearly comprehending? Here, a bhikkhu knows feelings as they arise, persist, and cease; perceptions as they arise, persist, and cease. Thus, bhikkhus, a bhikkhu is clearly comprehending.  

6. Dwell with clear comprehension and mindfulness—this is my instruction."  

36. Sixth: Awakening  

1-2. At Savatthi...  

3. "Bhikkhus, there are four establishments of mindfulness. What four?  

A bhikkhu contemplates the body in the body... feelings... mind... phenomena...  

4. For one who cultivates and frequently practices these, one of two fruits may be expected: either final knowledge here and now or, if residue remains, the state of non-returning."  

37. Seventh: Desire  

1. At Savatthi...  

2. "Bhikkhus, there are four establishments of mindfulness. What four?  

3. Here, a bhikkhu contemplates the body in the body... If he dwells thus, desire for the body is abandoned; abandoning desire, he realizes the deathless.  

4-5. Similarly for feelings... mind...  

6. Contemplating phenomena in phenomena... desire for phenomena is abandoned; abandoning desire, he realizes the deathless."  

38. Eighth: Full Understanding  

2. "Bhikkhus, there are four establishments of mindfulness. What four?  

3. Here, a bhikkhu contemplates the body... fully understanding the body; fully understanding it, he realizes the deathless.  

4-6. Similarly for feelings... mind... phenomena..."  

39. Ninth: Cultivation  

2. "Bhikkhus, I will teach the cultivation of the four establishments of mindfulness. Listen.  

How is it cultivated?  

3. Here, a bhikkhu contemplates the body... feelings... mind... phenomena...  

4. Thus, bhikkhus, cultivate the four establishments of mindfulness."  

40. Tenth: Exposition  

2. "Bhikkhus, I will teach the establishments of mindfulness, their cultivation, and the path leading to their cultivation. Listen.  

3. What are the establishments? A bhikkhu contemplates the body... feelings... mind... phenomena...  

4. What is their cultivation? Contemplating the origin of the body... its cessation... origin and cessation... Similarly for feelings... mind... phenomena...  

5. What is the path? The Noble Eightfold Path: right view... right concentration.  

Thus, bhikkhus, is the path leading to their cultivation."  

End of the Fourth Chapter: Unheard.  

Verses of Summary:  
Unheard, Dispassion, Loss,  
Cultivation, Mindfulness,  
Awakening, Desire, Full Understanding,  
Cultivation, Exposition—ten.

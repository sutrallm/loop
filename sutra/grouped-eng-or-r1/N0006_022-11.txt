1. The Chapter on Aspects  

103. First Aspect  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, there are four aspects. What four? The aspect of existence, the aspect of the origin of existence, the aspect of cessation, and the aspect of the path leading to the cessation of existence.  

4. Bhikkhus, what is the aspect of existence? It is the five aggregates subject to clinging. What five? The form aggregate subject to clinging, the feeling aggregate subject to clinging, the perception aggregate subject to clinging, the formations aggregate subject to clinging, and the consciousness aggregate subject to clinging. Bhikkhus, this is called the aspect of existence.  

5. Bhikkhus, what is the aspect of the origin of existence? It is the craving leading to renewed existence, accompanied by delight and lust, seeking delight here and there—that is, sensual craving, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence. Bhikkhus, this is called the aspect of the origin of existence.  

6. Bhikkhus, what is the aspect of cessation? It is the remainderless fading away and cessation of that craving, its abandoning, relinquishment, release, and non-attachment. Bhikkhus, this is called the aspect of cessation.  

7. Bhikkhus, what is the aspect of the path leading to the cessation of existence? It is the Noble Eightfold Path: right view... even up to... right concentration. Bhikkhus, this is called the aspect of the path leading to the cessation of existence.  

8. Bhikkhus, there are these four aspects."  

104. Second Suffering  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, I will teach you suffering, the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path leading to the cessation of suffering. Listen carefully.  

4. Bhikkhus, what is suffering? It is the five aggregates subject to clinging. What five? The form aggregate subject to clinging... even up to... the consciousness aggregate subject to clinging. Bhikkhus, this is called suffering.  

5. Bhikkhus, what is the origin of suffering? It is the craving leading to renewed existence... even up to... craving for non-existence. Bhikkhus, this is called the origin of suffering.  

6. Bhikkhus, what is the cessation of suffering? It is the remainderless fading away and cessation of that craving... even up to... non-attachment. Bhikkhus, this is called the cessation of suffering.  

7. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the cessation of suffering? It is the Noble Eightfold Path: right view... even up to... right concentration. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the cessation of suffering."  

105. Third Existence  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, I will teach you existence, the origin of existence, the cessation of existence, and the path leading to the cessation of existence. Listen carefully.  

4. Bhikkhus, what is existence? It is the five aggregates subject to clinging. What five? The form aggregate subject to clinging... even up to... the consciousness aggregate subject to clinging. Bhikkhus, this is called existence.  

5. Bhikkhus, what is the origin of existence? It is the craving leading to renewed existence... even up to... Bhikkhus, this is called the origin of existence.  

6. Bhikkhus, what is the cessation of existence? It is the remainderless fading away... even up to... Bhikkhus, this is called the cessation of existence.  

7-8. Bhikkhus, what is the path leading to the cessation of existence? It is the Noble Eightfold Path: right view... even up to... right concentration. Bhikkhus, this is called the path leading to the cessation of existence."  

106. Fourth To Be Fully Understood  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, [I will teach] the things to be fully understood, full understanding, and the one who fully understands. Listen carefully.  

4. Bhikkhus, what are the things to be fully understood? Bhikkhus, form is to be fully understood, feeling... perception... formations... consciousness is to be fully understood. Bhikkhus, these are called the things to be fully understood.  

5. Bhikkhus, what is full understanding? Bhikkhus, it is the destruction of lust, the destruction of hatred, and the destruction of delusion. Bhikkhus, this is called full understanding.  

6. Bhikkhus, who is the one who fully understands? It is the one known as arahant, the venerable of such a name and clan. Bhikkhus, this is called the one who fully understands."  

107. Fifth Ascetics (1)  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, there are five aggregates subject to clinging. What five? The form aggregate subject to clinging... even up to... the consciousness aggregate subject to clinging.  

4. Bhikkhus, ascetics or brahmins who do not truly understand the gratification, danger, and escape regarding these five aggregates subject to clinging—bhikkhus, I do not acknowledge them as ascetics among ascetics or brahmins among brahmins. Nor do these venerable ones realize for themselves in this very life the goal of the ascetic or brahmin life.  

5. Bhikkhus, ascetics or brahmins who truly understand the gratification, danger, and escape regarding these five aggregates subject to clinging—bhikkhus, I acknowledge them as ascetics among ascetics and brahmins among brahmins. These venerable ones realize for themselves in this very life the goal of the ascetic or brahmin life."  

108. Eighth Ascetics (2)  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, there are five aggregates subject to clinging. What five? The form aggregate subject to clinging... even up to...  

4-5. Bhikkhus, ascetics or brahmins who do not truly understand the origin, cessation, gratification, danger, and escape regarding these five aggregates subject to clinging... [those who do]... realize... themselves... the goal..."  

109. Seventh Stream-Enterer  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, there are five aggregates subject to clinging. What five? The form aggregate subject to clinging... even up to... the consciousness aggregate subject to clinging.  

4. Bhikkhus, when a noble disciple truly understands the origin, cessation, gratification, danger, and escape regarding these five aggregates subject to clinging, bhikkhus, this noble disciple is called a stream-enterer, no longer bound to the lower worlds, fixed in destiny, with enlightenment as their destination."  

110. Eighth Arahant  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, there are five aggregates subject to clinging. What five? The form aggregate subject to clinging... even up to... the consciousness aggregate subject to clinging.  

4. Bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu truly understands the origin, cessation, gratification, danger, and escape regarding these five aggregates subject to clinging, and is liberated through non-clinging, bhikkhus, this bhikkhu is called an arahant with taints destroyed, who has lived the holy life, done what had to be done, laid down the burden, reached his own goal, utterly destroyed the fetters of existence, and is liberated through final knowledge."  

111. Ninth Desire (1)  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, desire, lust, delight, and craving for form should be abandoned. When one abandons this form, it is like cutting off a palm tree at the root, making it non-existent, so that it cannot arise again in the future.  

4-6. Feeling... perception... formations...  

7. Desire, lust, delight, and craving for consciousness should be abandoned. When one abandons this consciousness, it is like cutting off a palm tree at the root, making it non-existent, so that it cannot arise again in the future."  

112. Tenth Desire (2)  

1-2. [At that time the Blessed One was dwelling in Savatthi...]  

3. "Bhikkhus, desire, lust, delight, craving, the grasp of clinging, mental adherence, present greed, and latent tendencies for form should be abandoned. When one abandons this form, it is like cutting off... a palm tree at the root... making it non-existent...  

4-5. Feeling... perception...  

6. Desire... for formations... abandoning...  

7. Desire, lust, delight, craving, the grasp of clinging, mental adherence, present greed, and latent tendencies for consciousness should be abandoned. When one abandons this consciousness, it is like cutting off a palm tree at the root, making it non-existent, so that it cannot arise again in the future."  

End of the Chapter on Aspects.  

The summary verse of this chapter:  

Aspects, Suffering, Existence,  
Full Understanding, Two Ascetics,  
Stream-Enterer, Arahant,  
And Two on Desire.

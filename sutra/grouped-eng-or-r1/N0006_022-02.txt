2. Chapter on Impermanence  

12. First: Impermanence  
1. Thus have I heard. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
2. Here... and so on...  
3. "Bhikkhus, form is impermanent... and so on...  
4. Feeling is impermanent...  
5. Perception is impermanent...  
6. Volitional formations are impermanent...  
7. Consciousness is impermanent. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus, they become disenchanted with form, disenchanted with feeling, disenchanted with perception, disenchanted with volitional formations, disenchanted with consciousness. Disenchanted, they become dispassionate. Through dispassion, they are liberated. In liberation, there arises the knowledge: ‘Birth is destroyed, the holy life has been lived, what had to be done has been done, there is no more coming to any state of being.’"  

13. Second: Suffering  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3-7. "Bhikkhus, form is suffering, feeling is suffering, perception is suffering, volitional formations are suffering, consciousness is suffering.  
8. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

14. Third: Non-Self  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3-7. "Bhikkhus, form is non-self, feeling is non-self, perception is non-self, volitional formations are non-self, consciousness is non-self.  
8. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

15. Fourth: The Impermanent (1)  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3. "Bhikkhus, form is impermanent. What is impermanent is suffering. What is suffering is non-self. What is non-self is not mine, not I, not my self. This should be seen with right wisdom as it truly is.  
4. Feeling is impermanent... (as above)...  
5. Perception is impermanent...  
6. Volitional formations are impermanent...  
7. Consciousness is impermanent... (as above)...  
8. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

16. Fifth: The Impermanent (2)  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3. "Bhikkhus, form is suffering. What is suffering is non-self. What is non-self is not mine, not I, not my self.  
4. Feeling is suffering...  
5. Perception is suffering...  
6. Volitional formations are suffering...  
7. Consciousness is suffering... (as above)... This should be seen with right wisdom as it truly is.  
8. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

17. Sixth: The Impermanent (3)  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3. "Bhikkhus, form is non-self. What is non-self is not mine, not I, not my self. This should be seen with right wisdom as it truly is.  
4-6. Feeling is non-self... Perception is non-self... Volitional formations are non-self...  
7. Consciousness is non-self... (as above)...  
8. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

18. Seventh: Cause (1)  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3. "Bhikkhus, form is impermanent. The cause and condition for the arising of form are also impermanent. Bhikkhus, how could form—arisen from an impermanent cause—be permanent?  
4. Feeling is impermanent... (as above)...  
5. Perception is impermanent...  
6. Volitional formations are impermanent...  
7. Consciousness is impermanent... (as above)...  
8. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

19. Eighth: Cause (2)  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3. "Bhikkhus, form is suffering. The cause and condition for the arising of form are also suffering. Bhikkhus, how could form—arisen from a suffering cause—be pleasant?  
4. Feeling is suffering...  
5. Perception is suffering...  
6. Volitional formations are suffering...  
7. Consciousness is suffering... (as above)... How could consciousness—arisen from a suffering cause—be pleasant?  
8. Bhikkhus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

20. Ninth: Cause (3)  
1-2. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
3. "Bhikkhus, form is non-self. The cause and condition for the arising of form are also non-self. Bhikkhus, how could form—arisen from a non-self cause—be self?  
4-6. Feeling is non-self... Perception is non-self... Volitional formations are non-self...  
7. Consciousness is non-self... (as above)...  
8. Bhikkhus, when a learned noble disciple sees thus... they know... ‘no more coming to any state of being.’"  

21. Tenth: Ananda  
1. At one time, the Blessed One was in Savatthi... and so on...  
2. Then the Venerable Ananda approached the Blessed One, paid homage, and sat to one side.  
3. He said: "Venerable sir, it is said, ‘cessation, cessation.’ By the cessation of what is cessation spoken of?"  
4. "Ananda, form is impermanent, conditioned, dependently arisen, subject to destruction, vanishing, fading away, and cessation. With the cessation of form, cessation is spoken of.  
5. Feeling is impermanent... (as above)...  
6-7. Perception is impermanent... Volitional formations are impermanent...  
8. Consciousness is impermanent... (as above)...  
9. Ananda, it is by the cessation of these things that cessation is spoken of."  

End of the Second Chapter on Impermanence  

Summary Verses:  
Impermanence, suffering, non-self,  
Three others on impermanence,  
Three on causes,  
And the tenth is Ananda's question.
